Privacy Policy

Last updated: January 30, 2025

Your privacy is important to us. This Privacy Policy explains how Pacer — your personal pace coach — handles your data. Pacer is designed to be simple and private: we only store your name for personalization, and everything else stays local on your device.

1. Information We Store

Personal Information

Pacer only stores minimal information for personalization:

  • Your Name: Used to personalize the app interface (e.g., "Hey [Name], let's smash our goals!")
  • App Preferences: Settings like subscription status and notification preferences

2. Data Processed Locally

Location Data

Pacer uses your device's location services for pace tracking, but this data stays on your device:

  • GPS Tracking: Real-time location is processed locally to calculate distance and pace
  • Route Creation: GPS coordinates are used to create workout routes saved to Apple Health
  • No Transmission: Your location data never leaves your device or Apple's secure systems

Health and Fitness Data

With your permission, workout data is saved directly to Apple HealthKit:

  • Workout Records: Duration, distance, and pace stored in Apple Health
  • Route Information: GPS routes associated with workouts in HealthKit
  • Local Storage: All health data remains in Apple's secure ecosystem

3. How We Use Your Information

The Only Data We Store

  • Your Name: Used for personalization in the app interface
  • App Settings: Preferences like Pro subscription status and notification settings

Everything Else Is Local

  • GPS data is processed on your device for real-time pace calculations
  • Workout data is saved directly to Apple Health, not our servers
  • Route information stays in Apple's secure HealthKit system
  • No personal data is transmitted to external servers

4. Data Storage and Security

Privacy by Design: Pacer is built to be private from the ground up. We don't have servers collecting your data because we don't need them.

Where Your Data Lives

  • On Your Device: Your name and app preferences are stored locally using iOS UserDefaults
  • Apple HealthKit: Workout and route data is saved directly to Apple's secure health platform
  • No Cloud Storage: We don't have databases or servers storing your personal information
  • No Data Mining: Since we don't collect your data, we can't (and don't want to) analyze or sell it

4. Third-Party Integration

Apple HealthKit

When you grant permission, Pacer integrates with Apple HealthKit to save your workout data. This data is governed by Apple's privacy policies and remains under your control.

5. Your Privacy Rights

Permission Control

  • Location Services: You can revoke location access in iOS Settings, though this will limit app functionality
  • HealthKit Access: Health permissions can be managed through iOS Health app settings
  • Data Deletion: You can delete workout data through the Health app or by uninstalling PacerApp

Data Portability

Your workout data stored in Apple Health can be exported using Apple's data export features, ensuring you maintain control over your fitness information.

6. Children's Privacy

Pacer is not intended for children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us to have it removed.

7. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will revise the "Last updated" date at the top of this policy. We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy periodically to stay informed about how we protect your information.

8. Contact Us

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy or how we handle your data, please contact us:

  • Website: simonngin.github.io
  • Through the "Contact us" feature in the Pacer settings

Summary: Pacer's privacy approach is simple: we only store your name for personalization. Everything else (GPS data, workouts, routes) stays local on your device or in Apple's secure systems. No servers, no data collection, no complicated privacy concerns.
